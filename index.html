COVID-10 Pandemic and Countries' Responses

Motivation

I decided to look at COVID data worldwide because I have been seeing and hearing the differences in COVID-19 situations among different countries through my international friends and my personal experience. I found this as a perfect opportunity to deepen my curiosity to understand how the virus spread depending on different measures that people take. Although it has become obvious that social distancing and wearing a mask are the most effective ways to prevent the virus, many of us know that not through evidence, but rather from media.

In this project, I would like to introduce several recaps of how the virus spreads and the impact of people’s particular behavior in increasing the number of cases, along with political measures taken by different countries. Some of my questions for the project included:

  • What were the differences in the infection trends worldwide?
  • What is likely to have impacted on the trends?
  • What makes the situation worse?
  • What should be done to stop the pandemic?

Scientific Prevention of COVID-19


According to Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), people get infected with COVID 19 through:

  • ● Through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs, sneezes or talks.
  • ● These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby or possibly be inhaled into the lungs.
To prevent the infection, wearing a mask and social distancing are extremely important. NHK, Japan’s public broadcaster, conducted an experiment using high-definition cameras and laser lighting. They had found that the movement of microdroplets, particles that are less than 100th of a millimetre in size, are emitted when we speak. These droplets float in the air for longer than 20 minutes and could easily lead infection if people are talking to each other at a normal distance.
In addition to being cautious to respiratory droplets, it is also crucial to avoid the virus on your hands and objects around you. CDC advises that we use a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.

COVID-19 Responses by Country

With the fundamental information how the virus spreads, we understand that social distancing and minimizing contacts are the key to suppressing the pandemic. Here, you can see the change in the number of cases in different countries and their corresponding political measures and major events regarding COVID-19 during a certain time period. These countries were selected to show both successful and unsuccessful handling of COVID-19. Generally speaking, the U.S and Switzerland experience the failure of controlling the virus while Ghana and Vietnam are one of the ideal examples of the response to COVID-19. China appears to have been also successful in handling the situation, but it is worth noting that some news source such as VOX EU claims that the data could be inaccurate due to the cover-ups by the Chinese local governments.
As a conclusion from examples of these countries, quick responses to the virus and potical measures that restrict people's contact seem to have great impact on the COVID-19 situation. The delays in the preventive measures cause failure in controlling the spread of virus.
